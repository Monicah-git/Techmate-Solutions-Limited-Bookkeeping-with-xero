01 · THE BUSINESS

A hardware retailer outgrowing its own paperwork

Context on the client, the books, and why the engagement mattered.

Techmate Solutions Ltd sells and services laptops — Acer, ASUS, Dell, HP, Lenovo and Samsung — to corporate accounts, schools and individual buyers across Kenya.

The business was moving real volume: dozens of live invoices, purchase orders running through three hardware distributors, a five-person payroll, and two active bank accounts. But the books hadn't kept pace with the growth. Revenue was coming in faster than it was being reconciled, supplier bills were being entered from more than one source, and nobody had a single place to see who owed the business money — or who the business owed.

I was brought in to set up and run the bookkeeping function inside Xero from the ground up: build a proper chart of accounts, bring receivables and payables under control, get payroll running on a predictable cycle, and turn the raw transaction history into financial reports the owner could actually use to make decisions.

02 · THE WORK

Rebuilding the books, one workflow at a time

Every screenshot below is pulled directly from the live Techmate Solutions Ltd workspace.
01
Foundation

Chart of accounts & financial setup

The first job on any bookkeeping engagement is the one clients never see: a chart of accounts that actually matches how the business operates. I structured accounts across Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Revenue and Expenses, coded each one, and set the correct tax treatment so every transaction downstream — invoices, bills, payroll, reports — lands in the right place automatically.

Why it matters — a clean chart of accounts is what lets a business owner trust their P&L on sight, instead of asking "but is this number actually right?"
02
Inventory

Product & inventory tracking

Because Techmate sells physical stock, cost of goods sold needed to tie back to real inventory, not guesswork. I maintained the products and services catalogue — cost price, sale price and quantity on hand for every laptop model across six brands — so margin on every sale could be checked at a glance and stock levels stayed visible.

03
Relationships

Customer & supplier records

A working set of contacts is what makes AR and AP chasing possible in the first place. I organized and maintained 57 contact records — 55 customers and 2 suppliers — complete with account numbers, emails and running balances, split into customer and supplier views with smart lists ready for aging and outstanding-balance follow-up.

04
Accounts Receivable

Invoicing & collections

I managed the full sales cycle — raising invoices, tracking approvals, and monitoring what was paid versus outstanding across 46 invoices. The sales dashboard made overdue exposure impossible to miss: KES 5.48M sitting in awaiting-payment invoices, concentrated in a small number of accounts.

Insight surfaced — three accounts (Innovative Tech, Green Valley Schools, Tech Support Ltd) accounted for the large majority of overdue receivables. That's the kind of finding a business owner can act on immediately — call three people, not forty-six.
05
Accounts Payable

Bills, purchase orders & a real save

On the payables side, I matched supplier bills against purchase orders raised with hardware distributors, kept 121 bills current, and reconciled staff reimbursements and payroll-linked entries separately from vendor payables so nothing got recorded twice.

Duplicate bills caught — Xero flagged 17 sets of potential duplicate bills sitting in the payables list. I reviewed each set line by line before anything was paid — catching double-entered supplier and staff bills that would otherwise have gone out as duplicate payments. This is the kind of control that protects cash, not just tidies records.
06
Expense Management

Coded, reimbursable, and audit-ready

Day-to-day spend — taxi fares, courier and shipping, printing, petty cash, office costs — was logged and coded to the correct expense account as it happened, each one tagged reimbursable and tied to a business purpose, so nothing sat unexplained at month end.

07
Payroll

Running payroll on a predictable cycle

I processed pay runs for a five-person team, keeping employee payments and statutory/other payments separated and consistent month over month, with every run closed out and marked paid on schedule.

Cash Flow

A single dashboard for cash in, cash out

With two active bank accounts and money moving in both directions daily, the owner needed one screen that answered "where do we stand right now?" — invoices owed to the business, bills due, and cash movement over time, all in one place.
